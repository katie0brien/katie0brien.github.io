About

Blending bubblegum and bullets like a hardcore Powerpuff Girl, Washington, D.C.-area rapper Rico Nasty weaves hip-hop with her self-described "sugar trap" aesthetic. While in high school, Rico Nasty began self-releasing content in 2014. Rico Nasty said that sugar trap "soft, beautiful, melodic, flowy vibes" as well as "trap music like Chicago drill music, Atlanta trap music, Memphis trap music, little bit of California trap music. I mix everything. If the sound catches my ear, I mix it."
